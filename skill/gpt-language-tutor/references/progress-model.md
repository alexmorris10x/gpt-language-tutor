# Progress model

Track individual communicative targets, not vague lesson completion.

## Target states

1. `New` — selected but not yet observed.
2. `Recognized` — understood with context, translation, or choices.
3. `Prompted` — produced after a model, partial frame, or direct cue.
4. `Independent` — recalled and used without language help in the practiced scenario.
5. `Transferred` — used correctly in a changed scenario or later lesson without language help.

## Evidence rules

- Advance only one state at a time unless the report contains direct evidence for a higher state.
- Recognition does not prove production.
- Repetition after a model is `Prompted`, not `Independent`.
- A correct answer to the changed retry may support `Independent` or `Transferred`, depending on how different the context is.
- Do not infer pronunciation quality from textual transcripts alone.
- Record uncertainty explicitly.
- A lesson may be complete even when some exposure items remain untested.

## Selecting the next lesson

- Carry two or three weak or useful prior items into a new scenario.
- Prefer spaced retrieval across later lessons to repeated identical practice within one lesson.
- If the learner needs help on most productive items, keep the next scenario but reduce the productive core or increase support.
- If the learner completes the main task independently and the changed retry with little support, move to the next scenario.
- Revisit `Independent` targets later before marking them `Transferred`.

## Log entry requirements

Every appended entry includes:

- date and duration;
- scenario and communicative task;
- exact examples used without help;
- exact examples used with prompting;
- listening and pronunciation evidence or `not observed`;
- errors and corrections that worked;
- target-state changes with reasons;
- next focus and review items.

Never rewrite older entries to make progress look cleaner.
